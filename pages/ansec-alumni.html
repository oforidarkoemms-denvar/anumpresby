A Son of Anum Who Shaped a Nation

From these classrooms to the highest offices of Ghana's government — one of ANSEC's most celebrated sons.

Distinguished Alumnus · Class of 1994

Hon. Thomas
Nyarko Ampem

Deputy Minister for Finance, Republic of Ghana

Born right here in Anum on 14th February 1978, Hon. Thomas Nyarko Ampem walked through the gates of ANSEC in 1992 — a young son of the Eastern Region with a curious mind and an instinct for public service. What ANSEC instilled in him became the foundation of a remarkable career that has taken him from a classroom in Anum to the floor of Ghana's Parliament and the Ministry of Finance.

Today he stands as one of Ghana's most powerful economic voices — proof that where you begin does not limit where you go when you carry ANSEC's spirit with you.

"Every cedi lost through corruption translates into unbuilt schools, unpaved roads, and lives adversely affected. We must uphold the highest standards — transparency, accountability, and service to the nation."
