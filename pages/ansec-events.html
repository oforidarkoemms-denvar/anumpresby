Prize-Giving & Speech Day

ANSEC's most prestigious annual ceremony brings together students, teachers, parents, alumni, and distinguished guests to celebrate academic achievement. Outstanding students across all year groups receive awards in the presence of the entire school community — a tradition that has marked the close of every academic year since the school's founding.

"The moment a student hears their name called at Speech Day — that is when they understand what it has all been for."

Inter-House Athletics & Sports Day

The athletics ground becomes an arena of fierce but joyful rivalry as ANSEC's four houses compete across sprint, relay, field events, and ball sports. Sports Day is the loudest and most colourful event in the ANSEC calendar — a celebration of discipline, teamwork, and physical excellence that draws the entire school community together.

"House colours fill every corner of the field. For one day, every student becomes an athlete and every teacher becomes a spectator."

Graduation & Farewell

Valedictory Service for Final-Year Students

The Valedictory Service is ANSEC's most emotionally significant event — the formal farewell to the graduating class. Students, in full uniform for the last time, receive blessings from the school chaplain, hear addresses from the headmaster and student leaders, and are sent into the world as ANSEC alumni. It is a moment of profound pride and gratitude.

"They leave as students. They return as alumni. The gates of ANSEC remain open to them always."

Since Our Founding

Enduring Traditions

Some events go beyond the calendar. These are the living customs that connect each generation of ANSEC students to those who came before them.

37
Morning Assembly
Since 1937

Every school day begins with a whole-school assembly — hymn, prayer, announcements, and a brief address. The rhythm that has opened every ANSEC day for over 80 years.

52
House System & Rivalry
Since 1952

The four houses compete year-round in academics, sport, and culture. House identity forms some of the strongest bonds ANSEC alumni carry throughout their lives.

68
Head Boy/Girl Inaugural Speech
Since 1968

The newly invested Head Boy and Head Girl each deliver a formal inaugural address to the whole school — a tradition that has produced some of Ghana's finest public speakers.

80
Cadet Corps Parade
Since 1980

The ANSEC Cadet Corps performs a formal parade on Founders' Day and during national observances — a tradition of discipline, precision, and patriotism that is uniquely ANSEC.
