Find Us

We're in Anum, Eastern Region

Easily reachable from Accra and Kumasi. Anum is a vibrant town on the Volta River — ANSEC sits at its educational heart.

Open in Google Maps
GPS Coordinates

Getting Here

1
Take the Accra–Kumasi Highway (N1) heading north from Accra.
2
Drive through Nsawam and continue towards Suhum.
3
At Atimpoku Junction, turn right (east) towards the Volta River crossing.
4
Cross the Adomi Bridge over the Volta River.
5
ANSEC is 2 km into Anum town on the main road — watch for the school signboard.
Approx. 2 – 2.5 hours from Accra
1
Head to Accra Central or Madina Terminal and board a trotro headed to Atimpoku or Anum.
2
Alight at Anum junction and take a motorbike taxi (okada) or walking 10 min to the school.
3
Direct Anum-bound trotros also depart from Adenta terminal on school visitation days.
Approx. 3 – 4 hours total travel
1
Book a Bolt or Uber intercity ride from Accra. Enter "Anum Presbyterian Senior High School, Anum" as your destination.
2
Agree on intercity fare upfront. Typical cost is GHS 180 – 260 from Accra depending on vehicle type.
3
The school gate is clearly marked. Ask the driver to call you if unsure — our gateman will assist.
Approx. 2 – 2.5 hours door to gate
1
From Kumasi, take the Kumasi–Accra Highway south-east through Konongo and Nkawkaw.
2
At Bunso Junction, branch right towards Atimpoku and the Volta River.
3
Cross Adomi Bridge, then follow signs into Anum — approximately 3 km to ANSEC.
Approx. 2.5 – 3 hours from Kumasi

FAQ

Common Questions

Quick answers to the questions we hear most often. Can't find what you need? Send us a message above.

Admissions to ANSEC are processed through the Ghana Education Service (GES) computerised school placement system (CSSPS). Prospective SHS 1 students are placed based on their BECE results. For transfers and special cases, contact our Admissions Office directly at [email protected] or call +233 (0) XXX XXX XXXX ext. 12.

School fees are due at the start of each term. Payment is accepted via GCB Bank direct deposit, mobile money (MTN, Vodafone, AirtelTigo), or in-person at the Finance Office. Fee schedules are communicated via the school notice board and WhatsApp parent groups each term.

Yes. Visitation days are scheduled each term — usually one Saturday per month. Parents and guardians are expected to sign in at the gate and use the designated visiting areas. Unscheduled visits during exam periods are discouraged. Consult the school calendar for the current term's visitation dates.

Transcript and certificate requests are handled by the Academic Affairs office. Submit a written request in person or via email to [email protected], attaching a copy of your ID and school attendance years. Processing takes 5 – 10 working days. There may be a small administrative fee.

We love hearing from our alumni! Email [email protected] with your name, year of graduation, and programme. You can also visit the Alumni Portal on our website to update your details, join the directory, and hear about homecoming events. The ANSEC Alumni Association is very active and welcomes all graduates.

ANSEC is a Presbyterian institution with a strong sense of decorum. Visitors are expected to dress modestly and respectfully. Revealing clothing, shorts, and sleeveless tops are discouraged on campus. Smart-casual attire is appropriate. Please notify the gateman of the purpose of your visit.
